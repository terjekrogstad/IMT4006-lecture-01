The memex (a portmanteau of "memory" and "index"[1] or "memory" and "extender") is the name of the hypothetical proto-hypertext system that Vannevar Bush described in his 1945 The Atlantic Monthly article "As We May Think".

Project Xanadu was the first hypertext project, founded in 1960 by Ted Nelson. Administrators of Project Xanadu have declared it an improvement over the World Wide Web, with mission statement: "Today's popular software simulates paper.

NLS, or the "oN-Line System", was a revolutionary computer collaboration system from the 1960s.
